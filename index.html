Shogun Prarie-Breaker

A brief description of my most recent bicyle build

FRAME

I bought the frame off a guy I found on Instagram. Its an early 1980's Shogun mountain bike frame. Although there are no markings on in, I assume its a "Prarie -Breaker" It has great tire clearance and can run both 26in wheels and 27.5.

COMPONENTS

The components are a mix match of parts from the part bin and random bikes I had around our place. I scored an early 90s Trek 930 from the Austin Bike Farm a few years ago for $10, a lot of the components came from that

Drive Train

3x10, XT Cassette, LX Crank, 9 speed Shimano derailleur, 10 speed shimano chain, front derailleur will be leaving soon and I'll be heading the 1x10 route.

Shifting

MicroShift Bar End Levers 2/3 x 10 speed.
I swapped the left for the right so that I could get the full ten gears out of my 9 speed derailleur. Once you get use to the indexing on the front gears, this has been a great hack for me.

Cockpit

Soma Portola bars with Newbaum's Cloth Bar Tape. Brake levers are some Dia Comps I got off an old bike. Saddle: Brooks B-17

Rollers

GEAX AKA 2.3 on CR-17 rims laced to Deore hubs.
